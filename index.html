CFO Notes — March 2026
Prepared by TurnkeyCFO

March was a difficult month for Heirloom Church. Revenue came in at $17.5K — less than half the 9-month average and 73% below February's strong $64K. The most likely explanation is timing: several large partner gifts that typically arrive in March appear to have been received early in February. We should confirm with the giving team whether this represents a permanent shift or a one-time acceleration.

On the expense side, $67.3K is the highest single month in the trailing 9. The spike appears driven by elevated facilities/supplies costs and a concentration of annual software renewals. Salaries remained in normal range. The combined effect — low revenue meeting high expenses — produced a $49.7K deficit that erases February's $18.8K surplus and then some.

The 9-month picture shows cumulative net at –$60.3K. While Heirloom's full-year Jan–Dec 2025 was roughly break-even, the trend since July has been consistently negative outside of February's outlier. Cash reserves are being drawn down.

Recommended Actions

  • Confirm with giving team whether partner gift timing has permanently shifted
  • Audit the March facilities/supplies spike — identify one-time vs. recurring charges
  • Review software renewals hitting in March; evaluate which subscriptions can be consolidated or eliminated (Mailchimp at $18K/yr remains the biggest opportunity)
  • Model April–June cash projection using conservative giving assumptions
  • Establish a 3-month operating reserve target of $99K and track progress monthly
